Introduction

Quantum computing harnesses the laws of quantum mechanics to solve problems too complex for classical computers. It uses qubits instead of bits, enabling parallel processing and exponential speedups.

How It Works

  • Qubits: Can be in multiple states at once (superposition).
  • Entanglement: Qubits become interlinked, affecting each other even when apart.
  • Quantum Gates: Operations applied to qubits for computation.
